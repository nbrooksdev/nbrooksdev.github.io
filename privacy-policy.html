Data we collect

Our services collect data automatically. Square the Circles is an exception and does not collect data automatically. Services collecting data may use cookies and similar technologies such as mobile SDKs. Automatically collected data may include:

  • Your IP address, which may be used to derive general location data.
  • User interactions including service launches and taps.
  • Diagnostic information including service crash logs, launch times, hang rates, and energy usage.
  • Device identifiers such as your device ID, MAC address, and IMEI. On Android devices, the advertising ID may also be collected.
  • Your device information including operating system, software versions, display resolution, browser data, system and language preferences, time zone, and device name.

We may also collect data that you provide to us directly. Any form of correspondence that you send us and the content of that correspondence may be collected. This includes marketplace reviews and email messages.

Your data is collected and processed to support our legitimate interests to operate and maintain our services. Where required by local law, your consent, if granted, will be our legitimate interest to collect and process certain data.

Your rights and options

Your rights and options regarding your personal data include:

  • The right to access your data. You may request to receive data about you that we have retained. You may also inquire about our storage and processing methods.
  • The right to correct your data. You may request that we correct inaccuracies and omissions in your personal data.
  • The right to erasure of your data. You may request that we permanently delete certain data about you.
  • The right to restrict processing of your data.
  • The right to portability of your data. You may request that we transfer your data to a data controller via a common, machine-readable format.
  • The right to object. You may object to continued processing of your data.
  • The right not to be subject to automated decision-making.
  • The right to opt-out of targeted advertising. Opt-out methods:
    • If prompted for consent within the service, respond that you do not consent or close the prompt with no selection. This method is only available where it is required by local law.
    • If "Privacy Options" is available in the menu within the service, navigate to "Menu" > "Privacy Options" and respond to the prompt that you do not consent or that you do not want your data sold or shared. The wording of the prompt will depend on local law. This method is only available where it is required by local law.
    • Within an ad window visible in the service, click on the AdChoices icon (the blue "i" within a triangle), select "Ad options", and toggle off "Personalize ads on this app". Select "Learn more" beneath "Personalize ads on this app" to visit a web page hosted by our advertising service provider detailing this option.
    • Prevent our services from accessing your device's advertising ID. Following are details by device operating system:
      • Apple's IDFA is the iOS advertising ID. By default, our services do not utilize the IDFA. Access to the IDFA can be managed for each service by opening "Settings" on your device and navigating to "Privacy and Security" > "Tracking".
      • Google's AAID is the Android advertising ID. Our services utilize the AAID unless access to this ID is restricted by the user. The AAID can be reset or deleted by opening "Settings" on your device and navigating to "Privacy" > "Ads". On devices with older Android software versions, the option to "Opt out of Ads Personalization" may appear instead of the option to delete the AAID. If a "Privacy" > "Ads" path does not appear in "Settings", an update to a newer Android software version may be required to use this method of opting out.
